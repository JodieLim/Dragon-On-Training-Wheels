Proficiencies

Proficiency Bonus: Trust Me, I'm a Pro!

To be proficient at something means to be good at something. You’re fluent in it. You know how to perform the task, and use any of the tools required. Just like in real life, you can be proficient in different things:
  • Armor
  • Weapons
  • Skills
  • Saving Throws
  • Tools

All characters have a “proficiency bonus” to something. This is a modifier that is added to rolls where the character is using a skill or making a save they are proficient in.
Think of it like this. If you’re good at something, there’s a better chance that you should succeed. The bonus helps your roll, and helps increase the chance of your character’s success!

Not proficienct?

If you are not proficient in a tool or a weapon, you simply don’t add your proficiency bonus. However, if you wear armor you are not proficient in, there are some consequences.

If you wear armor you are not proficient in...
  • You have disadvantage (you have to roll twice and take the lower number) on any roll that involves Strength or Dexterity
  • You cannot cast spells.
