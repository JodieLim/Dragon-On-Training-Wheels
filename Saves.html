Saves

Saving Throws:

Saving throws, or “saves”, represent your character trying to overcome a threat that is happening to them. Characters don’t decide to perform a saving throw; they are forced into doing so because something forced them into that situation.You perform a saving throw by rolling your dice and adding your modifier that corresponds with the save.

Every class is proficient in two different saves. If you are proficient in a save you are making, you’ll add the proficiency bonus to it!
The number you get is trying to overcome something called a “difficulty class”, or DC for short. If your value hits the number or above, then you pass!

A strength saving throw might be...

  • Trying to push debris or rubble off of you
  • Holding onto the ledge of a cliff

A dexterity saving throw might be…

  • Dodging out of the way of a spell or dragon’s breath
  • Trying to catch your footing after you fall

A constitution saving throw might be…

  • Resisting poison
  • Holding your breath

An intelligence saving throw might be…

  • Determining an illusion isn’t real with your knowledge of the subject
  • Resisting a psionic attack (more common at higher levels)

A wisdom saving throw might be…

  • Resisting an enchantment or charm (very common type of spell to resist!)

A strength saving throw might be...

  • Resisting possession from a ghost or other force
  • Resisting the power of a personality
