Skills

What are Skills?

Skills represent different aspects of each main attribute. If a character is proficient in a skill, that means they are better at that skill than average. Some of these skills might overlap, so it’s up to your DM to decide what check you need to make!

How do Skill Checks Work?

When your DM requests that you make a skill check, you will roll a D20 and add the modifier for the attribute that skill belongs to. For example, for an Athletics check, you will add your Strength modifier. If you are proficient in a skill, you will also add your proficiency bonus to the roll result.

Strength Skills

  • Athletics

    • Athletics covers anything that involves physical activity. You might make an athletics check to scale a cliff or swim across a river.

Dexterity Skills

  • Acrobatics

    • Acrobatics refers to stunts and balancing. You might make an acrobatics check to do a backflip or to stay balanced while the ground shakes.

  • Sleight of Hand

    • Sleight of hand refers to physical tricks and theft. You might make a sleight of hand check to perform a card trick or to pickpocket someone.

  • Stealth

    • Stealth covers any action that needs to stay hidden. You might make a stealth check to sneak out of an inn at night or to hide in the shadows of an alley.

Intelligence Skills

  • Arcana

    • Arcana refers to your knowledge about magic. You might make an arcana check to determine the ability of a magic item or to identify a spell someone else has cast.

  • History

    • History refers to your knowledge about the history of the world of your campaign. You might make a history check to remember the name of a long-dead king or to recall the outcome of an ancient war.

  • Investigation

    • Investigation covers any searching action. You might make an investigation check to look for clues in an old book or to find an escape route from a prison cell.

  • Nature

    • Nature refers to your knowledge about the natural world. You might make a nature check to decide if a plant is edible or to identify a wild animal.

  • Religion

    • Religion refers to your knowledge about the various religions of your campaign’s world. You might make a religion check to identify a holy symbol or to figure out the meaning of a prayer.

Wisdom Skills

  • Animal Handling

    • Animal handling covers any action involving animals. You might make an animal handling check to calm a frightened dog or to mount an unruly horse.

  • Insight

    • Insight refers to your ability to determine someone’s intentions. You might make an insight check when trying to figure out if a stranger is friend or foe or to see if a shopkeeper is willing to haggle.

  • Medicine

    • Medicine refers to your knowledge of health, illness, and remedies. You might make a medicine check to stabilize someone who is dying (link to death saves?) or to figure out if a rash is a sign of something serious.

  • Perception

    • Perception covers anything your character needs to notice. You might make a perception check to overhear a nearby conversation or to spot goblins lurking in the trees.

  • Survival

    • Survival refers to your ability to survive in nature. You might make a survival check to follow animal tracks or to build a shelter for the night.

Charisma Skills

  • Deception

    • Deception refers to your ability to hide the truth. You might make a deception check to lie on the witness stand or to convince someone they have never seen you before.

  • Intimidation

    • Intimidation refers to your ability to use overt or implied threats to persuade others. You might make an intimidation check to scare away townsfolk or to break up a fight.

  • Performance

    • Performance refers to your ability to entertain others. You might make a performance check to sing a song or to tell a ghost story.

  • Persuasion

    • Persuasion refers to your ability to influence others. You might make a persuasion check to negotiate a peace treaty or to get a lower price on a sword you want to buy.
