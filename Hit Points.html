Hit Points

Hit Points: Hit Me Baby One More Time!

Hit points determine how tough your character are. The more hit points, the more hits you can generally take. This is just like a health bar from any other game!:

Your hit points at first level is equal to the highest value of your hit point dice and your constitution modifier.

Example:

Hank is a sorcerer. His hit dice is a 1d6. His constitution is 12, which means his modifier is +1. Hank’s hit points at first level is 7, because 6 + 1 = 7!

After 1st level, you'll roll one of your hit dice and add the number you get, your constitution modifier, and your current hit point maximum.

Hit Point Dice: Hit Pointdexter

Hit point dice, or more commonly known as “hit dice”, determine what your hit points are. They also can be used to recover your health.

What value your hit point dice are depend on what your class are. Tankier units tend to have higher hit dice, (barbarians get a d12), and squishier units tend to get smaller hit dice (sorcerers get a 1d8).

After a rest, you can use hit dice to regain hit points. Throughout the game of DND, your adventuring party will rest between battle and other activities. When the DM says you are taking a rest, you can say you’re using your hit dice to heal yourself.
Hit dice are super important! Make sure when you build your character to record the type of hit dice they have, and the number of hit dice they have.
